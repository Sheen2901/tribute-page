Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of India's space and missile programs. He served as the chief project coordinator for India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and made significant contributions to the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

He was known as the "People's President" due to his humble and down-to-earth nature. Dr. Abdul Kalam was passionate about inspiring and motivating young minds, particularly students, and he frequently interacted with them, delivering speeches and sharing his knowledge and experiences.

Dr. Kalam was awarded several prestigious honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Padma Vibhushan. He also authored numerous books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire," which continues to inspire millions of readers worldwide.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His contributions to science, education, and the nation as a whole continue to inspire generations.

Legacy and Achievements

  • Played a key role in the development of India's space and missile programs.
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • Received several awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna.
  • Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds".
  • Inspired and motivated millions of people, especially the youth.
